Survival : Add your Survival Die to all non-combat tasks related to surviving in the wilds and wastes such as finding safe food, finding clean water, avoiding environmental hazards, identifying dangerous creatures, navigation through the wastes, etc.. In addition, the mutant can add their Survival Die to all Saves involving toxins, radiation, and environmental effects.
 
Character Mutations

All mutant characters have one or more mutations. Roll on table MU-2 (on Mutant Handout) to determine what type of mutation you have. Then roll to determine the sub-table under the type of mutation. Finally, roll on the sub-table to determine the mutation. Once you have determined what mutation you have, roll the percentage chance for each item listed. Yes, it is possible to receive no benefits or penalties for the mutation.

  • Hit Points: 1d6 per level
  • Weapons: A robot is trained in no conventional weapons, though it may use its various tools and attachments to fight with. As a robot gains levels, these weapons are improved.
  • Armor: None
  • Alignment: Neutral
  • Robot Nature
    • Robots cannot be healed, only repaired
    • At low levels, Robots are immune to mind affecting spells and effects, though as the robot advances, its positronic brain becomes more sentient, making it more vulnerable to such effects.
    • Robots suffer double damage from spells or effects that specifically damage metal or other inanimate substances.
    • Robots receive a -4 penalty to saving throws for spells or effects that specifically damage metal or other inanimate substances.
  • Technomancy: Appearing as somewhere between science and magic, robots wield powers (protocols) from ages beyond the ancestors of men
    • Robots receive two starting protocols>
    • Additional protocols must be found and integrated into the robot’s positronic brain, though as that brain expands through experience, robots receive additional memory with which to store these complex processes.
    • Patrons must be chosen from the supernatural beings from the dimension of Mechanix.
    • Manifestation of protocols is decidedly technological.
  • Esoteric Repair: A robot may repair itself, other robots, and even other technological devices, provided it has the materials available to do so.
    • Materials Needed
      • Technological Components: 1 pound
      • Raw Materials: 2 pounds
        • Metal
        • Tar
        • Glass
      • Combination of Technological Components and Raw Materials
    • A typical repair (up to 3 dice) requires 1 full turn of repair.
    • Required Levels of Repair: To repair technological devices, or to correct specific failures
      • 2 dice: Malfunction (Loss of subsystems such as ocular or auditory units, single limb, or minor malfunction/damage to any technological device)
      • 3 dice: Major Malfunction (Recover from deactivation, major positronic brain damage, multiple limb loss, magical damage to multiple components, full failure of any technological device)
      • 5 dice: Cybernetic Healing (healing a “meat unit” by technological means)
    • Cybernetic Healing: Using your Esoteric Repair function to “heal” living things
      • Available starting at 5th level
      • Requires Technological Components: 25 pounds
      • Heals up to 5 dice of hit points in the living thing
      • Causes a temporary loss of 1 Stamina point and 1 Personality point per application to the living thing’s body
      • On a malfunction (natural 1), the living thing takes 2d6 damage to hit points, and if still living, takes 1d3 permanent Stamina and Personality damage.

Hard To Kill :
 
Character Options: Choose two
 
The Sword of my Father: Some Savages come from famous lineages of heroes, shamans, tricksters, and the like, and often bear a token of that lineage, handed down through the generations. It could be a mighty sword, a lucky fetish, a golden torque, or any other object. When using this object, the Savage character may apply a +1d3 modifier to a single kind of roll (attack, Luck, a single type of save, etc.). However, the loss of the object will result in a loss of 1d6 Luck, permanently. The GM work with the player to determine what the item is, and also should determine what happens if the object is destroyed.
 
Companion Beast: You have an animal companion. The companion is a prime (see p. 382 of DCC Core Rulebook) of a mundane species such as a dingo, coyote, wolf, wildcat, bear, bird, horse, or other such creature of no more than 3HD. The creature is a boon companion, smarter than others of its ilk, and fiercely protective. But you must guard your companion’s back, for such friends are not bought but earned. The loss of an animal companion results in the loss of 2 points of Luck, permanently, and the savage character must quest for a new one.
 
Mighty Thews: You are incredibly strong for your size. You may roll an additional 1d6 for your Strength attribute and add the result permanently (up to 18 max).
 
Dancer's Grace: You are light on your feet and incredibly agile. You may roll an additional 1d6 for your Agility attribute and add the result permanently (up to 18 max).
 
Shaman: You have congress with mystical beings drawing upon their fell powers. You may learn Patron Bond and Invoke Patron, along with the relevant patron spells (as Wizard of equivalent level). You may bond more than one patron, but will be subject to all effects of patron taint and any other obligations incurred, and may not learn more spells than a wizard of your level, minus 1.

  • Hit Points:1d6 per level
  • Weapons: Daggers, Clubs, Staves, Hand Axes, Crossbows, Pistols and Rifles of any Era, Grenades and Explosives of any Era
  • Armor: Any
  • Alignment:
    • Neutral (common): Has unbiased nature of scientific understanding
      • Great at: Vehicle Repair
      • Good at: Use Alien Tech, Weapon Tinkering
      • Average at: Computer Use, General Tech
      • Okay at: Bot Repair
    • Lawful (uncommon): Obsessed with theoretical science
      • Great at: General Tech, Use Alien Tech
      • Good at: Computer Use
      • Average at: Bot Repair, Vehicle Repair
      • Okay at: Weapon Tinkering
    • Chaotic (uncommon): Prone to be called mad scientists
      • Great at: Weapon Tinkering
      • Good at: Bot Repair
      • Average at: Computer Use, Vehicle Repair, Use Alien Tech
      • Okay at: General Tech
  • Jury Rigging Skills
    • Weapon Tinkering DC Checks: With this you can repair damaged weapons so that they will work again, for a while
      • DC 5: Clearing gun jams, sharpening blades, and heavy maintenance
      • DC 10/15: Fixing broken weapons with kit bashed parts, DC changes depending on the severity of the damage and what parts are available
      • DC 25-35: Building a gun entirely out of scrap. You can also attempt to manufacture functional ammunition for guns
    • Bot Repair DC Checks: While creating a robot from scratch is beyond the ken of most people, you can fix and reprogram them with some success
      • DC 5: Reprogramming a simple servant bot
      • DC 10: Reprogramming a construction or manufacturing class bot; Fixing minor damage (less than 25% of the total hp for the bot) and requires access to good scrap; Salvaging useful parts from Bots
      • DC 15: Reprogramming a security and combat bot; Fixing major damage (between 25% to 75% of the total hp for the bot) also requires specialized parts, tools, and lots of time
      • DC 20+: Reprogramming a self aware bot; Bots damaged beyond 75% of their hp, depending on the damage done to them, again requiring specialized parts, tools, and lots of time
    • Computer Use DC Checks: Despite the variety of cosmic radiation and electromagnetic disturbances caused by the cataclysm, many computers survived. This skill allows you to attempt to hack into, utilize, and program computers you encounter
      • DC 10+: Programming a computer increases based on what the program does and how many computer resources it needs to run
      • DC 10-15: Accessing personal or public computers depending of the level of security running
      • DC 15-20: Accessing Mainframes and supercomputers depending of the level of security running
      • DC 20+: Accessing Self aware systems depending of the level of security running
      • If the computer is being actively monitored for suspicious activity, add +1d5 to the DC
    • Vehicle Repair DC Checks: Although many no longer run on petroleum fuels, motor vehicles are still widely used by many societies that can keep them functional. Despite not being considered “high science”, you are capable of acting as a mechanic, if only to earn room and board
      • DC 5: Basic vehicle maintenance
      • DC 10: Fixing minor damage (less than 30% of the total hp for the vehicle) and requires access to good scrap; Salvaging useful parts from Vehicles
      • DC 15-20: Fixing major damage (between 30% to 90% of the total hp for the vehicle) or changing what kind of fuel it uses requires specialized parts, tools, and lots of time
      • DC 20+: Vehicles damaged beyond 90% of their hp, depending on the damage done to them, again requiring specialized parts, tools, and lots of time
    • General Tech DC Checks: This is the catch-all category for any terrestrial technology that you might encounter that is not already covered by another skill
      • The DC for working on a device will depend on how complex the device is, how common the device is, and if it requires specialized parts or tools

    • Backstab: The most successful thieves kill without their victims ever being ware of the threat. Typically used in conjunction with Sneak, Hide or both.
      • When attacking a target from behind or when the target is otherwise unaware, the thief receives the indicated attack bonus to their attack roll.
      • On a hit, the thief automatically achieves a critical hit, rolling on the crit table as per their level
      • Certain weapons are particularly effective with backstab attempts and do additional damage, as noted in the equipment list.

      • DC 10: Stone surfaces
      • DC 15: Moderately noisy surfaces, such as creaking wooden boards
      • DC 20: Extremely noisy surfaces, like crackling leaves, still water, or crunchy gravel
    • Hide in Shadows DC Checks: A successful hide in shadows check means the thief cannot be seen. Check is not opposed.
      • DC 5: Hiding at night or in a shaded or dimly lit area
      • DC 10: Sneaking down a hallway with moderate cover (chairs, bookcases, crevasses, nooks and crannies, alcoves, etc.) or hiding under a full moon
      • DC 15: Hiding in daylight but in a dark shadow or behind a solid object
      • DC 20: Hiding in broad daylight with minimal obstruction
    • Pick Pocket DC Checks: The thief surreptitiously takes an object off a target’s person. This skill also includes other feats of legerdemain such as card tricks, minor magic tricks, and so on.
      • DC 5: Stealing from an unaware target with a loose pocket and an unsecured coin pouch
      • DC 6-19: Varying degrees of watchfulness in between the high and low
      • DC 20: Picking the pocket of a target that is actively watching and monitoring his or her belongings
    • Climb Sheer Surfaces DC Checks: Needs Thieves' tools
      • DC 10: A normal stone wall
      • DC 20: Perfectly smooth surface with no visible handhold
    • Pick Lock DC Checks: Needs Thieves' tools
      • DC 10: A mundane lock
      • DC 20: An extremely well crafted lock
      • DC 25+: Locks of legendary manufacture and notable difficulty
    • Find/Disable Traps DC Checks: A natural 1 on a disable trap check triggers the trap. Needs Thieves' tools
      • DC 10: A large, bulky trap. This would include traps like a pit in the floor, a spring loaded axe, or a dropped portcullis.
      • DC 15+: More subtle traps
    • Forge Documents DC Checks: Needs Thieves' tools.
      • DC 5-20: The DC varies with the complexity and originality of the source document.
    • Disguise Self DC Checks: The degree of change determines the DC.
      • DC 5: The thief can transform himself to resemble someone of the same basic race and physical dimensions.
      • DC 10: Changing significant facial features
      • DC 15: Changing physical traits, like mannerisms and height
      • DC 20+: Fooling someone close to the target (such as a parent or spouse)
    • Read Languages DC Checks:
      • DC 15: Interpreting simple meaning
      • DC 20: Interpreting anything more detailed
    • Handle Poison DC Checks: Needs Thieves' tools. On a natural 1 on any attack roll with a poisoned blade, the thief automatically poisons himself, in addition to any fumble results.
      • DC 10: Safety check. On a failure, the thief accidentally poisons themself! This check is made each time poison is applied to a blade or other surface.
    • Cast Spell from Scrolls DC Checks: Provided a spell is written on a scroll, a thief can attempt to read the scroll and cast the magical spell. The thief does not apply Intelligence or Personality modifiers to his roll, nor may he attempt spellburn.
      • DC Variable: The spell check DC is as standard for that spell, but the thief rolls the indicated type of die to attempt to beat that DC.

      • High Penalty: Interpreting anything more detailed
    • Handle Poison Checks: Needs Thieves' tools. On a natural 1 on any attack roll with a poisoned blade, the thief automatically poisons himself, in addition to any fumble results. DM will give penalties to roll depending on the poison being handled.
      • Variable penalty: Safety check. On a failure, the thief accidentally poisons themself! This check is made each time poison is applied to a blade or other surface.
    • Cast Spell from Scrolls DC Checks: Provided a spell is written on a scroll, a thief can attempt to read the scroll and cast the magical spell. The thief does not apply Intelligence or Personality modifiers to his roll, nor may he attempt spellburn.
      • DC Variable: The spell check DC is as standard for that spell, but the thief rolls the indicated type of die to attempt to beat that DC.

Wizard Abilities

  • Hit Points: 1d4 per level
  • Weapons: Dagger, longbow, longsword, shortbow, short sword, and staff
  • Armor: Wizards rarely wear armor, as it hinders spellcasting.
  • Alignment: Chaotic wizards study black magic. Neutral or lawful wizards seek control over elements. Wizards of all persuasions practice enchantment.
  • Languages: Knows two additional languages for every point of Int modifier
  • Action Dice: A wizard’s first action die can be used for attacks or spell checks, but his second action die can only be used for spell checks. At 5th level, a wizard can cast two spells in a single round, the first with a d20 spell check and the second with a d14. Note that the results of mercurial magic may modify the action dice based on the dice chain.
