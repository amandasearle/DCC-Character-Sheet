Halfling Abilities

Stealth Bonus :
 
  • Hit Points: 1d6 per level
  • Weapons: Club, dagger, handaxe, staff, short sword, crossbow, javelin, shortbow, and sling
  • Armor: Any
  • Alignment: Law (common), Neutral (uncommon), Chaos (extremely rare)
  • Languages: Knows one additional language
  • Two-weapon fighting
    • Attack Die: d16 + d16
    • Can fight with two equal sized weapons, such as two handaxes or two short swords
    • Scores a Critical and Automatic hit on a natural 16
    • Fumbles only when both dice come up a 1
    • If AGI >16, use normal two-weapon fighting rules
  • Infravision 30'
  • Good Luck Charm
    • Double the bonus of a Luck check
      • For every 1 point of Luck expended, gain a +2 to your roll
    • Recover lost Luck
      • Luck score is restored each night by a number of points equal to level
      • Luck score cannot be taken past natural maximum
    • Expend Luck for Allies
      • Ally must be nearby and visible
      • Can act out of Initiative order to burn Luck and apply to Ally's rolls
      • Luck Modifier can apply to any roll made by an Ally
      • This trait applies to only one halfling in a party. More halflings do not make the party more lucky.

Morlock Abilities

Acute Senses Check :
 
  • Hit Points: 1d10 per level
  • Weapons: Club, crossbow, dagger, handaxe, javelin, shortbow, short sword, sling, and staff
  • Armor: Any
  • Alignment: Law (uncommon), Neutral (common), Chaos (uncommon)
  • Languages: Knows one additional language
  • Claw Fighting
    • Must fight with both claws
    • Each claw gets its own attack die: d16
    • Only the Primary Claw scores a Critical and Automatic hit on a natural 16
    • Only the Primary Claw fumbles on a 1
    • Claw damage increases with level
    • If AGI >16, use normal two-weapon fighting rules
  • Burrow
    • Can only burrow through earth
    • Can burrow at a movement rate of 20'
    • Tunnel made is only for use by Morlocks
  • Underground Skills
    • Smell Gold/Gems
      • Strong concentration within 100'
      • Smaller concentrations, down to a single coin, as low as 40' (for a single coin or gem)
    • Find Construction: Bonus equal to Class Level
      • Detecting Traps
      • Detecting Slanting Passages
      • Detecting Shifting Walls
      • Detecting New Construction
  • Blind Luck
    • Can detect vibrations in the earth (since they are effectively blind)
    • Immune to visual illusions or anything dependent on sight
    • Acute Senses
      • Greater sense of smell, sound and vibrations
      • Once per session, can roll a Luck Check to automatically dodge an incoming surprise attack, notice an important clue, avoid a trap, or anything else the player can think of and the judge accepts.
    • Twice as difficult to catch by surprise in underground environments

Savage Abilities

Hard To Kill :
 
Character Options: Choose two
 
The Sword of my Father: Some Savages come from famous lineages of heroes, shamans, tricksters, and the like, and often bear a token of that lineage, handed down through the generations. It could be a mighty sword, a lucky fetish, a golden torque, or any other object. When using this object, the Savage character may apply a +1d3 modifier to a single kind of roll (attack, Luck, a single type of save, etc.). However, the loss of the object will result in a loss of 1d6 Luck, permanently. The GM work with the player to determine what the item is, and also should determine what happens if the object is destroyed.
 
Companion Beast: You have an animal companion. The companion is a prime (see p. 382 of DCC Core Rulebook) of a mundane species such as a dingo, coyote, wolf, wildcat, bear, bird, horse, or other such creature of no more than 3HD. The creature is a boon companion, smarter than others of its ilk, and fiercely protective. But you must guard your companions back, for such friends are not bought but earned. The loss of an animal companion results in the loss of 2 points of Luck, permanently, and the savage character must quest for a new one.
 
Mighty Thews: You are incredibly strong for your size. You may roll an additional 1d6 for your Strength attribute and add the result permanently (up to 18 max).
 
Dancer's Grace: You are light on your feet and incredibly agile. You may roll an additional 1d6 for your Agility attribute and add the result permanently (up to 18 max).
 
Shaman: You have congress with mystical beings drawing upon their fell powers. You may learn Patron Bond and Invoke Patron, along with the relevant patron spells (as Wizard of equivalent level). You may bond more than one patron, but will be subject to all effects of patron taint and any other obligations incurred, and may not learn more spells than a wizard of your level, minus 1.
 
  • Hit Points: 1d10 per level
  • Weapons: Dagger, club, spear, short sword, long sword, mace, battle axe, dart, hand axe, javelin, short bow, long bow, sling, blowgun, and staff
  • Armor: Any, but prefers going unArmored or with no better than Hide
  • Alignment: Law (uncommon), Neutral (common), Chaos (common)
  • Reasonable Armor
    • Wears No Armor
      • Armor bonus equal to Class Level
      • Additional armor bonus of +2
    • Wears Hide Armor or below
      • Armor bonus equal to Class Level
    • Wears Heavier than Hide Armor
      • No Armor Bonus
  • Child of Nature
    • +5 to Lore of Plants or Animals
    • +5 to Stealth checks outdoors
    • +5 to Climb checks
    • +5 to Find food or water outdoors
    • +5 to Tracking outdoors

If you have a class feature that also boosts your unArmored AC then select that here too.
